# Digital Marketing Campaign Analysis

This project analyzes digital marketing campaign performance using SQL.  
The goal is to evaluate channel efficiency, engagement, and conversion performance to support data-driven marketing decisions.

---

## Business Objective
To understand which marketing channels perform best in terms of:
- Cost efficiency
- Customer engagement
- Conversions

---

## Dataset Overview
The dataset contains customer-level marketing data including:
- Campaign channels and types
- Ad spend and conversions
- Website engagement metrics
- Email and social interaction metrics

---

## Key Business Questions
1. What is the total ad spend and total conversions across all campaigns?
2. Which marketing channel has the lowest and highest cost per conversion?
3. Which channels drive the highest website engagement?
4. Is higher spend always associated with better conversion performance?

---

## SQL Analysis Performed
The analysis includes:
- Total ad spend and conversions
- Cost per conversion (overall and by channel)
- Average click-through rate and conversion rate
- Website engagement metrics by channel

---

## Key Insights
- PPC has the lowest cost per conversion, making it the most cost-efficient channel.
- Email has the highest cost per conversion, indicating lower efficiency.
- Referral drives the highest website visits, showing strong engagement.
- Higher ad spend does not always lead to higher conversions.

---

## Recommendations
- Scale PPC campaigns due to strong cost efficiency.
- Optimize Email campaigns to improve conversion performance.
- Leverage Referral traffic by strengthening partnerships and referral programs.
